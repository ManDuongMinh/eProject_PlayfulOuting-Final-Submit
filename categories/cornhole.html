Cornhole

How to play

To play cornhole, two teams of one or two players each stand at opposite ends of a cornhole board, 27 feet apart, and take turns tossing bean bags underhand towards the opposing board, aiming to get them through the hole for 3 points or to land on the board for 1 point; the first team to reach 21 points wins, using "cancellation scoring" where the higher scoring team's points cancel out the lower scoring team's points in each round.

Set up:

Place two cornhole boards 27 feet apart on flat ground, with the holes facing away from each other.

Teams:

Play with two teams of one or two players each.

Throwing

: Each player throws their bags underhand from behind the front edge of their board, aiming for the opposite board's hole.

Scoring:

A bag through the hole counts as 3 points. A bag landing on the board counts as 1 point.
