Côn Đảo (or Con Dao Islands)

Côn Đảo (or Con Dao Islands) is an archipelago off the coast of Bà Rịa – Vũng Tàu province, Vietnam. It's about 97 nautical miles from Vũng Tàu city and comprises 16 islands and islets. Côn Đảo is known for its pristine beauty, with crystal-clear beaches, fine white sand, and a diverse ecosystem. It's also famous for its historical sites related to the resistance against French colonialism and American imperialism.

Natural Beauty:

Beaches: Côn Đảo boasts numerous beautiful beaches, such as Đầm Trầu beach, Nhát beach, Ông Đụng beach, and others.
Ecosystem: The archipelago has a rich forest and marine ecosystem, particularly diverse coral reefs.
Côn Đảo National Park: This park is a sanctuary for many rare flora and fauna, including sea turtles.

Historical Sites:

Côn Đảo was once a place where revolutionary soldiers and political prisoners were detained during wartime.
Notable historical sites include the Côn Đảo prison, the "tiger cages," and Hàng Dương cemetery, which attract many visitors.

Tourist Activities:

Visitors can engage in activities like snorkeling to see coral reefs, fishing, and exploring the pristine forests.
Côn Đảo is also an ideal destination for relaxation and enjoying a peaceful and fresh atmosphere.
During the sea turtle nesting season, tourists can participate in sea turtle conservation activities.

Top Locations:

Dam Trau beach
Hon Bay Canh islands
Con Dao prison
Con Dao museum

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to explore Côn Đảo is from March to September. Although it's during the rainy season, the sea is calm, and there are gentle winds in the eastern and northeastern islands.
